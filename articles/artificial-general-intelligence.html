Artificial General Intelligence

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to systems capable of flexible, human-level reasoning across domains.

Conceptual Definition

AGI describes intelligence that can learn, reason, and adapt across tasks without task-specific retraining. Unlike narrow AI, AGI generalizes knowledge across domains.

Architectural Challenges

AGI requires memory, abstraction, causal reasoning, and long-term planning. No existing system fully integrates these capabilities.

Alignment and Control

Ensuring AGI objectives align with human values is a central research challenge, involving safety, interpretability, and governance.

Future Implications

AGI could reshape science, economics, and governance. Its development represents a civilizational inflection point.

Enkyclo treats AGI as a theoretical boundary condition for intelligence, not a guaranteed outcome.
